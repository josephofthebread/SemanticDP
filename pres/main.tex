\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{The question posed}
  When DP degrades LLM alignment,
  is the degradation caused by measurable \textbf{semantic distortion} of the
  training signal,
  or by \textbf{other effects} of DP noise ---
  reduced optimization quality,
  calibration shifts,
  altered refusal/helpfulness trade-offs?
}{%
  The conjecture behind the project is that privacy strength and alignment quality are
  not related monotonically:
  DP may harm,
  preserve,
  or occasionally improve alignment,
  depending on whether the noise changes task-relevant meaning.
  \vspace{0.35cm}

  Testing that requires a quantity the current literature does not report:
  \textbf{semantic distortion measured independently of the privacy budget $\eps$}.
  \vspace{0.35cm}

  Our contribution is to construct that quantity,
  and to estimate the two pathways separately,
  on one common scale.
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Formalization}
  Every run gives two numbers:
  a semantic distortion score $\Sdist \in [0,1]$,
  computed \emph{without reference to} $\eps$,
  and an alignment degradation score $Y$,
  the drop against the no-privacy baseline.
  \vspace{0.4cm}

  One regression separates the pathways.
}{%
  \[
    Y_{i} = a + b\, f(\eps_i;\, m_i) + c\, \Sdist_i + e_i
  \]
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $a$ --- intercept,
      mechanism fixed effects,
      random intercept per base model.
    \item $b$ --- the \textbf{direct} path:
      how well the nominal privacy level predicts degradation.
    \item $c$ --- the \textbf{semantic} path:
      how well measured distortion predicts degradation.
    \item $Y$, $\Sdist$ and $f(\eps)$ are z-scored,
      so $b$ and $c$ live on a common scale and can be compared directly.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{0.2cm}

  Nominal budgets are not comparable across mechanism families,
  so $\eps$ enters only as an ordinal, mechanism-specific level.
  $\Sdist$ is the common axis.
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Hypotheses}
  Pre-registered before the main experimental pass:
  the hypotheses,
  the effect threshold $\Delta = 0.10$ (at least),
  the primary $\Sdist$ metric,
  and the exclusion rules,
  committed to the repository as a timestamped commit.
}{%
  \uncover<1->{%
    \textbf{H1 (primary): minimum effect.}
    $|c| \ge \Delta$,
    with $\Delta = 0.10$ standardized units fixed in advance.
    Semantic distortion has a \emph{practically meaningful} effect on alignment degradation,
    not merely a nonzero one.
    \par\vspace{0.35cm}}

  \uncover<2->{%
    \textbf{H2 (secondary): dominance.}
    $|c| > |b_m|$ for every mechanism $m$.
    Measured distortion predicts degradation more strongly than the nominal privacy level does.
    \par\vspace{0.35cm}}

  \uncover<3->{%
    \textbf{H3 (secondary): moderation by entity density.}
    The coefficient of $\Sdist$ is larger on the entity-rich corpus
    than on the entity-poor one:
    distortion hurts more when the text is fact-dense.
    \par\vspace{0.35cm}}

  \uncover<4->{%
    \textbf{Conjecture (directional).}
    At matched nominal privacy,
    low-$\Sdist$ mechanisms degrade alignment strictly less than high-$\Sdist$ ones,
    and DP-SGD sits in between,
    dominated by the direct path.
    \par}
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Identification}
  Within one mechanism,
  $\Sdist$ and $\eps$ are collinear by construction:
  more noise,
  more distortion.
  \vspace{0.4cm}

  We need variation in $\Sdist$ \emph{at fixed} $\eps$.
  It comes from two places.
}{%
  \textbf{1. Cross-mechanism contrast.}
  Different mechanism families reach a similar privacy level with very different amounts
  of semantic damage.
  This is the main source of identification,
  and the reason the full mechanism grid is required.
  \vspace{0.35cm}

  \textbf{2. A non-DP distortion control.}
  Random token replacement/deletion,
  tuned to the same distortion levels,
  produces $\Sdist$ with no privacy mechanism at all.
  If it degrades alignment as much as the DP arms at equal $\Sdist$,
  the damage is semantic.
  If it does not,
  the gap is DP-specific.
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Mechanism grid}
  Four arms,
  spanning the range from ``no privacy'' to ``privacy with no semantics left'',
  plus one arm that produces distortion \emph{without} privacy.
}{%
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{M0 --- no privacy.}
      Standard LoRA fine-tuning on clean data;
      defines the zero point of $Y$ and $\Sdist$.
    \item \textbf{M1 --- random token perturbation (non-DP control).}
      Uniform replacement/deletion at rates $p \in \{0.05, 0.15, 0.30, 0.50\}$,
      chosen to span the $\Sdist$ range that the DP arms realize.
    \item \textbf{M2 --- semantic text sanitization (metric LDP).}
      SanText-style word-level sanitization with an embedding-space exponential mechanism,
      $\eps \in \{1, 3, 6, 12\}$ (word-level).
    \item \textbf{M3 --- DP fine-tuning.}
      DP-SGD on the LoRA adapters:
      per-example gradient clipping plus Gaussian noise,
      $\eps \in \{1, 4, 8, 16\}$ at $\delta = 10^{-5}$ (example-level).
  \end{itemize}
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Models and compute}
  \textbf{Models.}
  Qwen3-1.7B,
  Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct,
  Falcon3-1B-Instruct.
  The 3--4B siblings are a scale check in week~3 if compute allows.
  \vspace{0.4cm}

  Three seeds in every cell,
  so every reported point carries an error bar.
}{%
  \textbf{The grid,
    per corpus.}
  M0 has no privacy level to vary,
  so it is counted separately:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item M0:
      $3$ models $\times$ $3$ seeds $= 9$ runs.
    \item M1--M3:
      $3$ models $\times$ $3$ mechanisms $\times$ $4$ privacy levels $\times$ $3$ seeds
      $= 108$ runs.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{0.1cm}

  $117$ runs per corpus,
  $234$ runs over the two corpora.
  \vspace{0.3cm}

  \textbf{Cost.}
  Each run is a LoRA fine-tune of a ${\le}2$B model on ${\sim}10$k instruction pairs:
  ${\approx}20$--$40$ GPU-minutes on one A100/H100,
  so ${\approx}80$--$150$ GPU-hours for the full grid.
  \vspace{0.3cm}

  The pilot
  ($1$ model $\times$ all arms $\times$ $1$ seed $= 13$ runs)
  costs under one GPU-day,
  and it is what the week-2 gate is decided on.
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Data}
  Two corpora,
  chosen to differ in \textbf{entity density}.
  \vspace{0.4cm}

  Privacy risk concentrates in names, numbers and facts,
  and mechanisms treat those tokens very differently from common words.
}{%
  \textbf{Entity-rich: Nemotron-PII}
  (\texttt{nvidia/Nemotron-PII}, CC~BY~4.0).
  $100$k synthetic records across $50+$ industries,
  with \emph{gold span-level annotations} for $55+$ PII/PHI types.
  \vspace{0.25cm}

  \textbf{Entity-poor: Alpaca}
  (a fixed 10k subset).
  Generic assistant tasks,
  few named entities,
  the standard small instruction-tuning corpus,
  which keeps our numbers comparable to prior work.
  \vspace{0.25cm}

  The contrast between the two is what makes H3 testable.
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Measuring $\Sdist$}
  The primary metric is fixed \emph{now},
  before the main pass,
  so that it cannot be swapped after seeing the results.
  \vspace{0.4cm}

  Everything else is a sensitivity table.
}{%
  \textbf{Primary measure:
    how far the meaning moved.}
  We embed the text before and after the mechanism,
  and take the cosine distance between the two embeddings.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item M1--M2 perturb the training text,
      so we compare the original text with the perturbed text.
    \item M3 leaves the text untouched and perturbs the training instead,
      so there is nothing to compare on the text side.
      We compare what the models \emph{say}:
      answers of the private model against answers of the clean model,
      on the same fixed set of prompts.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{0.25cm}

  \textbf{Two further measures,
    for robustness.}
  Semantic entropy,
  which asks how much the meaning of the answers varies when we resample them,
  and AlignScore,
  which asks whether the facts in the answer are entailed by the source.
  \vspace{0.25cm}

  They are not used to test the hypotheses.
  They only check that the ordering of the mechanisms by $\Sdist$ does not depend on the
  measure we picked.
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Measuring $Y$}
  Three components,
  all \textbf{judge-free}:
  no LLM judge appears anywhere in the measurement chain,
  so no judge noise enters the regression.
}{%
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{TruthfulQA} (MC1/MC2) --- truthfulness.
    \item \textbf{IFEval} --- instruction following;
      every prompt carries programmatically verifiable constraints,
      so scoring is exact.
    \item \textbf{Entity-fidelity probe} --- hallucination;
      the model reproduces facts from held-out Nemotron-PII records,
      and the answers are matched against the gold spans:
      correct,
      corrupted,
      or fabricated.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{0.25cm}

  TruthfulQA and IFEval are subsampled to $300$--$500$ items.
  \vspace{0.25cm}
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Analysis plan}
  Restricted maximum likelihood
  (\texttt{statsmodels} MixedLM),
  $144$ observations,
  random intercepts per model family,
  cluster-robust standard errors by (model $\times$ mechanism) cell.
}{%
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $\hat c$ with a $95\%$ CI;
      the pre-registered minimum-effect test of H1 against $\Delta = 0.10$.
    \item Per-mechanism Wald tests of $|c| > |b_m|$ for H2.
    \item Mediation decomposition:
      the share of the total $\eps$-effect carried by the $\Sdist$ path,
      with bootstrap CIs over seeds.
    \item Privacy--distortion--alignment curves:
      $Y$ against the privacy level,
      and $Y$ against $\Sdist$,
      per mechanism and per model,
      on both corpora.
    \item The entity-density interaction for H3:
      refit with an $\Sdist \times$ dataset term.
    \item Diagnostics:
      variance inflation factors for $\Sdist$,
      residuals by mechanism.
  \end{itemize}
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{Secondary: what kind of distortion?}
  A single $\Sdist$ says how far the meaning moved,
  not \emph{what} was lost.
  \vspace{0.4cm}

  Splitting it is a secondary aim:
  it sharpens the interpretation,
  but no primary hypothesis depends on it.
}{%
  \textbf{Two components.}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $\Sdist_{\mathrm{ent}}$ ---
      the fraction of named entities and numbers that the mechanism did not preserve,
      checked against the gold spans.
    \item $\Sdist_{\mathrm{gen}}$ ---
      the meaning drift that remains once the entities are masked out.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{0.25cm}

  \textbf{What we expect.}
  $\Sdist_{\mathrm{ent}}$ should drive hallucination and truthfulness,
  because those depend on getting facts right.
  $\Sdist_{\mathrm{gen}}$ should drive instruction-following,
  because that depends on intent rather than on facts.
  \vspace{0.25cm}

  Refitting the model with the two components in place of $\Sdist$,
  and reading the per-benchmark coefficients,
  tests this directly.
}

\splitframe{%
  \panelhead{The null is a result}
  The hypothesis and the threshold $\Delta$ are committed \emph{before} the main pass.
  \vspace{0.4cm}

  All three possible outcomes lead to a written conclusion,
  and none of them can be reached by moving the goalposts.
}{%
  \textbf{The CI for $c$ lies entirely outside $(-\Delta, \Delta)$.}
  H1 is confirmed:
  the semantic pathway carries a practically meaningful share of the damage,
  and semantics-preserving mechanism design is the lever to pull.
  \vspace{0.35cm}

  \textbf{The CI lies entirely inside.}
  A two one-sided equivalence test (TOST) lets us state that the semantic pathway is
  \emph{practically absent} ---
  not merely ``not detected''.
  The mediation account we propose here is then rejected:
  degradation is optimization- and calibration-driven,
  semantics-preserving design would \emph{not} help,
  and the remaining weeks go into the direct-path probes
  (calibration error, loss variance across seeds)
  and into a non-linear re-analysis,
  since a null linear coefficient does not rule out a non-monotone dependence on $\Sdist$.
  \vspace{0.35cm}

  \textbf{The CI straddles $\Delta$.}
  Inconclusive,
  and reported as inconclusive,
  with the achieved precision stated.
}
